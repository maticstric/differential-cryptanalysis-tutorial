Differential Cryptanalysis Tutorial

Abstract

Differential cryptanalysis is a subtle, beautiful, and powerful method of breaking some of the worldâ€™s strongest ciphers. This tutorial is a detailed and interactive introduction to differential cryptanalysis for novices with an interest in cryptography. Unlike other currently available tutorials, it avoids needless math notation, makes very few assumptions about prior knowledge, does not gloss over details, and is written to be understood by motivated highschool students, undergraduates in engineering/computer science, and amateur cryptographers.

Before continuing, know that there are two major prerequisites:

  • You must be comfortable with binary and hexidecimal notation, especially how to convert between them
  • You need a surface-level understanding of the purpose and use of symmetric-key block ciphers

It would also be helpful to have an understanding of the XOR operation, though that can be picked up as you read along.
